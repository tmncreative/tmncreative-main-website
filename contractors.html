FAQ

Common questions from contractors and builders.

Answers to what we hear from GCs, remodelers, and custom builders when they reach out to TMN Creative.

How much does a contractor website cost?

Contractor web design at TMN Creative starts at $2,250 for a Starter Refresh, $3,750 for a Full Site Rebuild (multi-page site with deep portfolio, project case studies, financing, licensing pages, BuilderTrend or CoConstruct integration, reviews, local SEO), and $5,000+ for Custom Studio Builds (custom home builders, multi-location remodelers, commercial contractors, design-build firms with deep portfolios). Flat fees, no retainers. Optional site care plans run $39 to $179 per month for monitoring, updates, and on-call edits.

Do you integrate with BuilderTrend, CoConstruct, and Procore?

Yes. TMN Creative integrates with BuilderTrend, CoConstruct (now part of BuilderTrend), Procore, JobTread, Houzz Pro, Jobber, and most modern construction project management platforms. Prospective clients can request consultations, submit project briefs, upload inspiration photos, or sign up for client portals directly into your CRM. We also wire in CallRail call tracking, Houzz lead routing, Angi pro lead pull-through, and Zapier or Make automations for any platform we don't natively support.

How do you handle portfolio depth?

Portfolio depth is the single highest-converting element on a contractor website. Buyers shop on proof of work, not promises. TMN Creative builds full project case-study pages (brief, design, build, finished result), filterable galleries by category (kitchen, bath, addition, custom build, commercial), before/after split sliders, image-schema markup, lazy-loading for fast page speed, and we help you commission drone footage and final-walkthrough video if your portfolio is thin or the photography is not telling the story. Every project page is a self-contained sales pitch.

Should the site show licensing, bonding, and insurance?

Yes, prominently. License number, bonding info, GL/WC insurance, BBB rating, and any specialty certifications (NARI, NAHB, NKBA, EPA RRP, OSHA 30) build immediate trust with both homeowners and commercial buyers. TMN Creative builds dedicated licensing and credentials pages, includes them in footer trust strips, marks them up with structured data, and can pull live BBB and Angi badges. Verifiable credentials also make contractor pages easier for AI and search systems to evaluate and summarize.

How long does it take to build a contractor website?

First draft in 72 hours. Most contractor sites are live in 7 to 10 days. Custom home builders and commercial contractors with deep portfolios run 2 to 3 weeks. Trevor and Taylor Niemann work directly on every project from our studio in Fort Worth, Texas, which is why turnaround is so fast and quality is consistent. Photo curation can extend timeline; we can also commission drone footage if your portfolio needs help.

Do you support financing for big remodel and custom build projects?

Yes. TMN Creative integrates financing CTAs and pre-qualification flows for Synchrony, GreenSky, Wells Fargo, Service Finance Company, Hearth, Acorn Finance, and other home improvement lenders. Financing closes high-ticket remodels (kitchen rebuilds, full additions, basement finishes, custom homes). We design financing pages like a checkout flow with monthly-payment calculators, soft-pull pre-qual, and clear CTAs. Construction loans and 203(k) information can be added for custom-home builders and renovation specialists.

Can the site capture both residential and commercial buyers?

Yes. Residential and commercial buyers shop differently and need different funnels. TMN Creative builds them as separate landing-page paths with their own CTAs, case studies, and follow-up flows. Commercial gets capability statements, COI on demand, account-management contact, project-history docs, bonding details, and references. Residential gets long-form project case studies, financing, designer bios, and consultation booking. The same site serves both audiences without the two cannibalizing each other for clicks or attention.

Do you replace Houzz, Hibu, or BlueCorona contractor sites?

Yes, frequently. Most of those platforms ship templated contractor sites with monthly retainers, restricted code access, and conversion patterns that have not been refreshed in years. TMN Creative hand-codes every site so it is leaner and faster, looks distinct (not templated), and is fully owned by you. We handle the migration, redirects, SEO continuity, Houzz Pro lead-routing rewires, and any other vendor handoffs. Most cutovers can be planned around redirects, metadata, and Search Console monitoring to protect search visibility during launch, and the new site has a stronger technical foundation for search because of speed, redirects, and structured data.
