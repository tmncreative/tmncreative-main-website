Buyer Intent

What usually goes wrong with dental template sites

The issue is not effort. Most practices signed up with a vendor years ago because it was easy. The practice has simply outgrown it.

01

Template sameness

Patients comparing three practices see three versions of the same layout. Nothing about the site says this practice is different.

02

Buried new-patient flow

Booking, insurance acceptance, financing, and first-visit expectations should be one tap away, not scattered across menus.

03

Weak local SEO structure

Implants, Invisalign, cosmetic, and emergency care each deserve a real page built around how patients search, not one crowded services list.

04

Vendor lock-in

Cancel the subscription and the website disappears. The practice paid for years and owns nothing.

05

Generic stock everything

Stock smiles and template copy undercut a practice that has real providers, real results, and real reviews to show.

06

Slow mobile experience

Most patients find a dentist on their phone. Heavy template platforms make the first impression slower than it should be.

FAQ

Questions practices ask before they book.

How much does a dental website redesign cost?

Dental website redesigns start at $2,250 for a focused homepage refresh, $3,750 for a full practice site rebuild with treatment pages and booking paths, and $5,000+ for cosmetic-forward or multi-location builds. Flat fees, no retainers.

Can you migrate our content from ProSites, Officite, or another dental template vendor?

Yes. We reuse the copy, photos, provider bios, and treatment details worth keeping, rewrite what is generic, and rebuild the site as hand-coded pages you own. Leaving the vendor no longer means losing the website.

Will we lose our Google rankings when we leave the template vendor?

No. We map every existing URL to the new site with 301 redirects, preserve titles and metadata that are working, rebuild schema, and submit the updated sitemap so rankings transfer instead of resetting.

Can patients book online through Dentrix, Eaglesoft, or Open Dental?

Yes. We route online booking through NexHealth, LocalMed, or the scheduler already connected to your practice management system, and keep marketing-site forms free of protected health information so sensitive intake stays in covered systems. For the broader trust playbook, see our healthcare practice website design guide.

How fast can a dental website redesign launch?

First draft is usually ready in 72 hours. Focused rebuilds often launch in 7 to 10 days after content and approvals. Multi-location groups typically run 2 to 3 weeks.
