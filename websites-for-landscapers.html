FAQ

Common questions from landscapers.

Answers to what we hear from landscape designers and maintenance company owners when they reach out.

How much does a landscaping website cost?

Landscaping web design at TMN Creative starts at $2,250 for a Starter Refresh, $3,750 for a Full Site Rebuild (multi-page site with portfolio, service pages, recurring service signups, service area pages, Aspire or LMN integration, and SEO), and $5,000+ for Custom Studio Builds (multi-location maintenance and design-build firms). Flat fees, no retainers, no monthly hosting lock-in. Optional site care plans run $39 to $179 per month if you want us handling updates and tweaks ongoing.

How important are portfolio galleries for a landscape design site?

Portfolio depth is the #1 conversion driver for landscape design-build firms. Homeowners shopping for $50,000+ landscape projects compare 3 to 5 firms by browsing portfolios first, almost always at night, almost always on a phone. Every site we build for landscapers includes a structured portfolio system: full-resolution before/after sliders, scope notes, design intent, materials, location, square footage, and budget tier. Each project gets its own URL with schema markup so it ranks for hyper-specific searches like "backyard pool patio Fort Worth" or "front yard xeriscape design Texas."

Do you integrate with Aspire, LMN, or Jobber?

Yes. We integrate the marketing site with Aspire, LMN (Landscape Management Network), Jobber, ServiceAutoPilot, SingleOps, RealGreen, Service Pro, and most modern landscape and lawn care software. Estimate forms and recurring service signups push directly into your operations system, so your team does not have to manually re-enter inquiries. We also wire up call tracking via CallRail or WhatConverts and Google Local Services Ads (LSA) where applicable, so you can see which channels actually drive booked work.

Can the website handle seasonal service shifts?

Yes. We build seasonal service modules that switch the homepage hero, CTAs, and featured services based on the date or weather. In spring, the site leads with cleanups, mulch, and design consults; in summer, lawn care and patio installs; in fall, leaf removal, aeration, and overseeding; in winter, snow removal and holiday lighting. This is built as a simple admin toggle so your team can switch seasons without calling a developer — just flip the season in the dashboard and the whole site adjusts.

Can the site sell recurring contracts and maintenance plans?

Yes. We build dedicated landing pages for recurring lawn care, fertilization programs, full-service maintenance, irrigation startup/winterization, and snow contracts. Online enrollment with recurring billing via Stripe, ServiceAutoPilot, RealGreen, or your software's native subscription module turns the site into a real revenue engine. Recurring revenue from the website is forecastable, predictable, and reduces sales-team workload — the customer onboards themselves through a multi-step form instead of waiting on a salesperson callback.

How long does it take to build a landscaping website?

First draft of the homepage in 72 hours. Full landscaping sites are typically live in 7 to 10 days. Custom Studio Builds for design-build firms with deep portfolios and multi-service content take 2 to 3 weeks. Most landscapers are live well before their next peak season — letting you capture spring or fall demand at full strength. We also handle photo curation, scope-note rewriting, and SEO copy in that timeline so you do not have to write the content yourself.

Can my landscaping website be understood by ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews?

Yes. Every landscaping site we build is optimized for AI search (AIO) alongside traditional SEO. Structured data (Service, LocalBusiness, ProfessionalService, FAQ schema), question-and-answer content blocks, properly cited service area information, and crawlable architecture mean ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews can better understand your business when someone asks "who is the best landscape designer in Fort Worth?" — a growing part of how homeowners research design-build options.

Do you replace WordPress, Squarespace, or Surefire Local landscape sites?

Yes — frequently. Most landscape sites we replace are WordPress with bloated themes, Squarespace, GoDaddy builders, Surefire Local, Scorpion, or Hibu templates. Those platforms ship cookie-cutter sites with monthly hosting lock-in and limited code access. Our hand-coded sites load much faster, look distinct (not templated), are built for stronger Core Web Vitals, and are fully owned by you. We handle the migration, redirects, and SEO continuity to protect as much existing visibility as possible during the move.
