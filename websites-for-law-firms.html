What's Built In

Built into every law firm site we ship.

Law firm websites have unique constraints — bar advertising rules, attorney-client confidentiality concerns, and prospective clients in distress who need answers fast. Our approach respects all of that, by default.

01

Bar-rule-aware copy

Copy is written to comply with ABA Model Rule 7.1-7.5 and your state's analogous rules (Texas Disciplinary Rules 7.01-7.05, California Rules 7.1-7.5, NY 7.1-7.5). We use approved disclaimers, structure case results carefully, and include required "attorney advertising" disclosures where applicable.

02

Attorney bios with credentials

Each attorney gets a clean bio page with bar admissions, education, JD/LLM, areas of practice, professional associations, awards, recognitions, publications, and a real headshot. Schema-tagged as Person/Attorney.

03

Practice area pages

Each practice area gets its own page — not a dropdown. We write copy that addresses the actual client concern (cost, timeline, what happens next) rather than legalese. This is what gets indexed, understood, and converted.

04

Case management integration

Direct routing to Clio, MyCase, PracticePanther, Smokeball, Filevine, RocketMatter, CosmoLex, Lawmatics, and Captorra. Contact forms route directly to your intake. We do not store client information on the marketing site.

05

Case results & testimonials

Where bar rules permit, we display case results and client testimonials with required disclaimers ("prior results do not guarantee a similar outcome"). Some jurisdictions (FL, NY, TX) have stricter rules — we follow them.

06

Local + AI search SEO

LegalService and Attorney schema, local SEO for Google Business Profile, Apple Maps, Bing Places, plus FAQ schema, llms.txt, and entity-rich copy for ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. Increasingly how prospective clients find attorneys.

07

Accessibility (WCAG 2.2 AA)

Color contrast, keyboard nav, screen reader semantics, focus states, form labels. Plus an accessibility statement page. Law firms have ADA exposure too — we build to reduce avoidable accessibility risk.

08

Mobile-first performance

Hand-coded HTML/CSS. No WordPress, no Justia, no FindLaw template. Strong mobile Core Web Vitals target. Built for fast mobile loads on 4G — which is what prospective clients use when they need an attorney now.

09

You own everything

Code, copy, design files, domain, hosting access — yours on day one. No proprietary CMS lock-in. If you ever leave, you take the site.

FAQ

Common questions from law firms.

Answers to what we hear from attorneys and firm administrators when they reach out.

How much does a law firm website cost?

Law firm web design at TMN Creative starts at $2,250 for a Starter Refresh, $3,750 for a Full Site Rebuild (multi-page firm site with attorney bios, practice areas, case results, intake routing, and bar-rule-aware copy), and $5,000+ for Custom Studio Builds (multi-attorney firms, plaintiff PI shops). Flat fees, no retainers. Optional site care: $39 to $179/month.

How do you handle ABA Model Rule 7 and state bar review?

Our copy is written to be compliance-aware. We avoid prohibited language (guarantees, comparative claims without substantiation, undefined superlatives like "best lawyer"), use approved disclaimers ("prior results do not guarantee a similar outcome"), structure testimonials per ABA Model Rule 7.1-7.5 and your state's analogous rules (Texas Disciplinary Rules 7.01-7.05, California Rules 7.1-7.5, etc.), and include "attorney advertising" disclosures where required. Final compliance review is your firm's responsibility — but our copy is structured to make first review faster and cleaner.

Do you integrate with Clio, MyCase, PracticePanther, and other case management?

Yes. We integrate with Clio, MyCase, PracticePanther, Smokeball, Filevine, RocketMatter, CosmoLex, Lawmatics, Captorra, Salesforce, and HubSpot. Contact forms route directly to your case management or CRM. We do not store client information on the marketing site, and we do not display anything that could create attorney-client confusion or be characterized as legal advice.

Can you display case results and client testimonials?

Yes — within the bounds of your jurisdiction's rules. We include required disclaimers, structure case results to avoid prohibited comparisons, and label testimonials per applicable rules. Some jurisdictions (Florida, New York, Texas) have stricter testimonial and result advertising rules — we account for them in the draft and confirm final language with counsel and provide your firm with a compliance review document for your CLO or marketing officer.

How long does it take to launch a law firm website?

First draft in 72 hours. Most firm sites are live in 7 to 10 days. Custom Studio Builds for multi-attorney firms with extensive practice areas, attorney bios, and bar-rule review take 2 to 3 weeks.

Do you replace Justia, FindLaw, LawLytics, or Scorpion sites?

Yes — frequently. Most attorney-focused platforms ship templates with monthly hosting lock-in, slow load times, and limited code access. Our hand-coded sites load much faster, look distinct (not templated), and are fully owned by you. We handle the migration, redirects, and SEO continuity. We have replaced Justia, FindLaw, LawLytics, Scorpion, Martindale, and Avvo Pro sites for clients.

Do you do law firm SEO and AI search optimization?

Yes. Every law firm site we ship includes traditional SEO (LegalService and Attorney schema, local SEO for Google Business Profile, Apple Maps, optimized meta tags, fast Core Web Vitals, semantic HTML, internal linking, practice area landing pages) and AI search optimization (FAQ schema, llms.txt, entity-rich copy structured for ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews). Prospective clients increasingly search for attorneys through AI assistants — we build pages that are easier for those systems to parse and summarize accurately.
